About Me

My Passions & Interests

Skiing

I started skiing when I was very young, but the sport truly became a central part of my life when I moved to Wausau. Living so close to the hills allowed me to transition from a casual hobbyist to a dedicated athlete, eventually joining my high school’s race team. Skiing is meaningful to me because it combines the technical precision of finding the perfect line with the raw, outdoor energy of the mountain, it’s a rare space where you are constantly challenged by both the clock and the elements.

Over the past several years, I have pushed myself to master every aspect of the mountain, from the high-speed discipline of racing to the high-stakes responsibility of being a ski patroller. I successfully balanced the rigors of competitive racing with the demanding training required for mountain safety, a challenge that taught me how to stay composed in high-pressure situations. A major achievement for me has been continuing my service as a ski patroller at Spirit Mountain while pursuing my degree at UMD, where I manage medical emergencies and safety protocols in sub-zero Duluth winters.

My experience on the slopes directly informs my goals in the MIS field by emphasizing the importance of risk management, systems thinking, and real-time problem-solving. Being a patroller has taught me that whether you are managing a network or a mountain, attention to detail is non-negotiable for safety and efficiency. In the future, I hope to combine my technical MIS skills with my passion for the winter sports industry, perhaps by developing more efficient data systems for resort operations or safety management.

Lacrosse

I discovered lacrosse in 6th grade when I moved to Wausau, Wisconsin. Looking for a way to break the ice and make new friends in a new town, I picked up a long pole and quickly found that the sport was the perfect bridge to building lasting connections. What started as a social outlet in middle school has since grown into a competitive college pursuit.

Over the years, I have anchored myself as a defenseman, though my current chapter at UMD Duluth has seen me transition into the high-speed role of a Long Stick Middie (LSM). While I am currently working hard as a backup and aiming for that first "pole goal," the experience has taken me across the country to compete in places like Utah and Ohio. Even though my gear setup remains true to my close-defense roots, playing the transition game in the MCLA has pushed my versatility on the field.

Lacrosse has been the defining constant in my life, teaching me the value of persistence and team chemistry. Whether I’m texting my buddy playing D1 for advice or grinding through scout team reps in Duluth, the sport remains my primary way of building a community. In the future, I hope to transition back to my natural home on the close-defense crease, bringing the speed and stick skills I've honed at LSM back to the heart of the defense.
